In-Hospital Outcomes of Variceal Haemorrhage in Patients With and Without Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (NIS 2017–2022)

Analysis for RCOP NIS IM4

Author
Affiliation

Dua Ali

Dow University of Health Sciences

Preamble:

  • Reference Papers:

    • Leiva et al. 2023
    • Yan et al. 2015

Study Objective: To compare in-hospital outcomes and complications between variceal hemorrhage patients with and without myeloproliferative neoplasms (MPNs) in U.S. hospitals, adjusting for demographic, socioeconomic, clinical, and hospital-related factors.

Data Source: Cross-sectional analysis using the National Inpatient Sample (NIS) database, a representative sample of all-payer inpatient hospitalizations in the U.S., from 2017 to 2022.

Patient Selection: Included all inpatient admissions for patients 18 years or older with a primary or secondary diagnosis of variceal hemorrhage, identified using ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes (I85.11 and I85.01). From this cohort, patients were categorized into two groups based on the presence or absence of myeloproliferative neoplasms. Patients with essential thrombocythemia (ICD-10 D47.3), polycythemia vera (ICD-10 D45), and primary myelofibrosis (ICD-10 D47.1, D75.81, and D47.4) were identified using ICD-10 codes.

Outcomes of Interest:

  • In-hospital Mortality

  • Blood Transfusion

  • ICU Admission

  • TIPS Procedure

  • Length of Stay (days)

  • Inflation-adjusted total hospitalisation costs (2022 USD, CPI-adjusted)

Statistical Analysis: Univariable and multivariable analyses were conducted to evaluate the association between myeloproliferative neoplasms and inpatient outcomes among variceal hemorrhage hospitalizations:

  • Univariable Analysis:

    • Continuous variables: Design-based Kruskal-Wallis test.

    • Categorical variables: Pearson’s X² test with Rao & Scott adjustment to account for survey design.

  • Multivariable Analysis:

    • Logistic regression for binary outcomes (e.g., mortality, blood transfusion, ICU admission, and TIPS procedure).

    • Linear regression for continuous outcomes (e.g., length of stay, total charges).

  • Adjustments: Models controlled for the following covariates:

    • Demographics and Socioeconomic Factors: Calendar year (continuous, 2017–2022), age (years), sex, race/ethnicity, median household income quartile, and primary expected payer.

    • Clinical Factors: A summary measure of comorbidity (Elixhauser comorbidity index), Hepatic encephalopathy, Ascites, and Splenomegaly.

    • Hospital-Level Factors: Hospital bed size, Hospital location and teaching status.

Software: All analyses were performed using R Statistical Language (Version 4.5.0; R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria), incorporating survey-weighted procedures via the survey package to account for the complex sampling design of NIS.
