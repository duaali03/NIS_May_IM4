In-Hospital Outcomes of Variceal Haemorrhage in Patients With and Without Myeloproliferative Neoplasms (NIS 2017–2022)

Analysis for RCOP NIS IM4

Author
Affiliation

Dua Ali

Dow University of Health Sciences

Preamble:

  • Reference Papers:

    • Leiva et al. 2023

    • Yan et al. 2015

  • Study Objective: To compare in-hospital outcomes and complications between variceal hemorrhage patients with and without myeloproliferative neoplasms (MPNs) in U.S. hospitals, adjusting for demographic, socioeconomic, clinical, and hospital-related factors.

  • Data Source: Cross-sectional analysis using the National Inpatient Sample (NIS) database, a representative sample of all-payer inpatient hospitalizations in the U.S., from 2017 to 2022.

  • Patient Selection: Included all inpatient admissions for patients 18 years or older with a primary or secondary diagnosis of variceal hemorrhage, identified using ICD-10-CM diagnosis codes (I85.*). From this cohort, patients were categorized into two groups based on the presence or absence of myeloproliferative neoplasms. Patients with essential thrombocythemia (ICD-10 D47.3), polycythemia vera (ICD-10 D45), and primary myelofibrosis (ICD-10 D47.1, D75.81, and D47.4) were identified using ICD-10 codes.

  • Outcomes of Interest:

    • In-hospital Mortality

    • Blood Transfusion

    • ICU Admission

    • TIPS Procedure

    • Length of Stay (days)

    • Inflation-adjusted Total Charge ($) [adjusted to 2022 dollars, using CPI data by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics]

  • Statistical Analysis: Univariable and multivariable analyses were conducted to evaluate the association between MPN and inpatient outcomes among variceal hemorrhage hospitalizations:

    • Univariable Analysis:

    • Categorical variables: Pearson’s X² test with Rao & Scott adjustment to account for survey design.

    • Continuous variables: Design-based Kruskal-Wallis test, a non-parametric alternative suitable for survey-weighted data

    • Multivariable Analysis:

    • Binary outcomes (e.g., in-hospital mortality, blood transfusion, ICU admission, TIPS procedure): Survey-weighted logistic regression using the quasibinomial family.

    • Continuous outcomes (length of stay, total charges): Survey-weighted linear regression using the gaussian family.

    • Results reported as adjusted odds ratios (aORs) or beta coefficients with 95% confidence intervals

    • Adjustments: Models controlled for the following covariates:

    • Demographics and Socioeconomic Factors: Age, Sex, Race, Residential income, Expected primary payer.

    • Clinical Factors: A summary measure of comorbidity (Elixhauser comorbidity index), Hepatic encephalopathy, Ascites, and Splenomegaly.

    • Hospital-Level Factors: Hospital region, Hospital bedsize, Hospital location and teaching status.

  • Software: All analyses were performed using R Statistical Language (Version 4.5.0; R Foundation for Statistical Computing, Vienna, Austria), incorporating survey-weighted procedures via the survey package to account for the complex sampling design of NIS.
